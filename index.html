About Lorraine Paquette

Author. Founder. Guide.

Her work centers on a quiet, often unspoken moment in a woman's life — the moment when what once fit no longer feels true.

Through her writing, small group circles, community spaces, and guided 1:1 support, she offers a grounded path back to self — not through reinvention, but through remembering.

Her debut book, A Quiet Return, reflects this philosophy: that transformation does not always arrive loudly or all at once, but instead unfolds in subtle, honest moments of recognition.

As the founder of The Quiet Return and The Quiet Becoming, her work extends beyond the page — creating spaces where women can slow down, listen inward, and reconnect with their own inner knowing.

She does not position herself as someone with answers, but as someone who walks alongside — holding space for what is real, what is changing, and what is ready to emerge.

Her work speaks to the woman who has lived a full life on the outside… and is now ready to live one that feels true on the inside.

This is not about becoming someone new. It is about returning to who you have always been.

This is not about becoming someone new.
It is about remembering who you were before the world asked you to be someone else.

This body of work is devoted to the quiet return to self.

It exists for the woman who has spent years meeting expectations, holding roles, and carrying what was asked of her — and is now sensing that something no longer fits.

Rather than pushing toward dramatic change, this work honors a different path: one of slowing down, listening inward, and recognizing what is already true.

Across writing, guided spaces, and conversation, the work supports a process of gentle disentangling — from roles, from patterns, from ways of being that were once necessary, but are no longer aligned.

At its core, this work is not about transformation as performance. It is about truth as lived experience.

A Quiet Return is the doorway. Becoming is the unfolding.

And everything offered is in service of helping women come home to themselves — in their own time, in their own way.

If something in you recognizes this… you are already on the path.
There is nothing you need to force. Only something to return to.
