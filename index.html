About Lorraine Paquette

Author. Founder. Guide.

Lorraine's work centers on a quiet, often unspoken moment in a woman's life — the moment when what once fit no longer feels true.

Through her writing, small group circles, community spaces, and guided 1:1 support, she offers a grounded path back to self — not through reinvention, but through remembering.

Her debut book, A Quiet Return, reflects this philosophy: that transformation does not always arrive loudly or all at once, but instead unfolds in subtle, honest moments of recognition.

As the founder of The Quiet Return and The Quiet Becoming, her work extends beyond the page — creating spaces where women can slow down, listen inward, and reconnect with their own inner knowing.

She does not position herself as someone with answers, but as someone who walks alongside — holding space for what is real, what is changing, and what is ready to emerge.

Her work speaks to the woman who has lived a full life on the outside… and is now ready to live one that feels true on the inside.

This is not about becoming someone new. It is about returning to who you have always been.
